Mindanao Fish Species

Discover the unique aquatic life of the southern Philippines

About Mindanao's Marine Biodiversity

Mindanao, the second-largest island in the Philippines, is surrounded by several important bodies of water including the Sulu Sea, Celebes Sea, and Philippine Sea. This geographical advantage contributes to its rich marine biodiversity, hosting hundreds of fish species that are both economically and ecologically important.

The waters around Mindanao are particularly known for their tuna resources, with General Santos City being dubbed the "Tuna Capital of the Philippines." Beyond commercial fishing, the region also boasts diverse coral reef ecosystems, especially in the Sulu Archipelago and the Davao Gulf.

Zamboanga Peninsula

The Zamboanga Peninsula is surrounded by the Sulu Sea to the west and the Moro Gulf to the east, making it a strategic location for fishing activities.

Curacha (Spanner Crab)

Though technically a crab, this seafood delicacy is a specialty of Zamboanga and is often included in local seafood directories.

Location: Zamboanga City

Bangsi (Flying Fish)

Known for its ability to leap out of water and glide over the surface, this fish is common in the waters of the Zamboanga Peninsula.

Location: Sulu Sea

Lapu-Lapu (Grouper)

A high-value fish found in the coral reefs around the peninsula, particularly in the waters of Basilan Strait.

Location: Basilan Strait

Northern Mindanao

Northern Mindanao faces the Bohol Sea and is known for its diverse marine ecosystems, from the deep waters of Iligan Bay to the coastal areas of Misamis Oriental.

Bariles (Yellowfin Tuna)

A large tuna species that's commercially important in the region, often caught in the waters of the Bohol Sea.

Location: Bohol Sea

Matambaka (Mackerel Scad)

A small pelagic fish that's commonly caught in the waters around Misamis Oriental and Camiguin.

Location: Misamis Oriental, Camiguin

Bolinao (Anchovy)

Small but economically important, bolinao is often dried and used as a condiment or side dish.

Location: Iligan Bay

Davao Region

The Davao Region faces the Philippine Sea to the east and encompasses the Davao Gulf, which is known for its rich marine biodiversity.

Tangigue (Spanish Mackerel)

A popular fish for grilling and kinilaw (Filipino ceviche), found in the waters of Davao Gulf.

Location: Davao Gulf

Malasugi (Marlin)

A large, predatory fish with a distinctive spear-like bill, found in the deeper waters of the Philippine Sea.

Location: Philippine Sea

Salay-Salay (Mackerel)

A small mackerel species that's commonly caught in the waters of Davao Gulf.

Location: Davao Gulf

SOCCSKSARGEN

SOCCSKSARGEN is home to General Santos City, the "Tuna Capital of the Philippines," and is known for its significant contribution to the country's tuna industry.

Tambakol (Skipjack Tuna)

A commercially important tuna species in the region, often caught in the waters of Sarangani Bay.

Location: Sarangani Bay

Tulingan (Mackerel Tuna)

A medium-sized tuna species that's commercially important in the region, often caught in the waters of the Celebes Sea.

Location: Celebes Sea

Gulyasan (Yellowfin Tuna Juvenile)

The juvenile form of yellowfin tuna, commonly caught in the waters around SOCCSKSARGEN.

Location: Celebes Sea

Caraga

The Caraga Region faces the Philippine Sea and is known for its diverse coastal ecosystems, including mangroves, seagrass beds, and coral reefs.

Danggit (Rabbitfish)

A popular breakfast fish, often dried and served with vinegar. It's abundant in the shallow waters around Surigao del Norte.

Location: Surigao del Norte

Kitong (Spotted Scat)

A brackish water fish found in mangrove areas and estuaries, common in the coastal areas of Agusan del Norte.

Location: Agusan del Norte

Alimango (Mud Crab)

While not a fish, mud crabs are an important part of the aquaculture and seafood industry in the Caraga region.

Location: Surigao del Sur

Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM)

BARMM encompasses several island provinces and mainland areas with rich coastal and marine resources, including the Sulu Archipelago and parts of Mindanao facing the Celebes Sea.

Sugpo (Giant Tiger Prawn)

Although a crustacean, prawns are a significant aquaculture product in BARMM, especially in areas like Maguindanao.

Location: Maguindanao

Kawakawa (Frigate Tuna)

A smaller tuna species commonly found in the waters around the Sulu Archipelago.

Location: Sulu Archipelago

Tëmaginting (Silver Carp)

A carp species commonly found in the waters around Magindanao communities in Central Mindanao.

Location: Maguindanao

About Luzon's Marine Biodiversity

Luzon, the largest island in the Philippines, is surrounded by several bodies of water including the West Philippine Sea and Sibuyan Sea. This geographical advantage contributes to its rich marine biodiversity, hosting hundreds of fish species that are both economically and ecologically important.

The waters around Luzon are home to various habitats including coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrass beds, which support diverse fish populations. From the deep-sea fishes of Batanes to the freshwater species of Laguna de Bay, Luzon offers a fascinating glimpse into the Philippines' aquatic treasures.

Ilocos Region

The Ilocos Region, located in the northwestern part of Luzon, is known for its rich coastal waters that support both artisanal and commercial fishing activities.

Cagayan Valley

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Central Luzon

The Central Luzon region is home to productive fishing grounds in the West Philippine Sea and inland areas.

CALABARZON

CALABARZON is rich in both freshwater and marine species, supported by Taal Lake, Laguna de Bay, and coastal waters in Batangas and Quezon. The region is home to iconic fish like Tawilis, Maliputo, Lapu-Lapu, Dilis, and Maya-Maya. Its coral reefs and seagrass beds also support marine life such as Sea Turtles and Whale Sharks, making it a key area for both fishing and conservation.

MIMAROPA

MIMAROPA, made up of Mindoro, Marinduque, Romblon, and Palawan, is known for its pristine waters and rich marine biodiversity. The region is home to coral reefs, seagrass beds, and mangroves that support species like Danggit (Rabbitfish), Lapu-Lapu (Grouper), Maya-Maya (Snapper), Sea Turtles, Dugong, and Butanding (Whale Shark). Palawan’s waters are especially important for marine conservation and sustainable fishing.

Bicol Region

The Bicol Region is surrounded by rich fishing grounds like the Albay Gulf and Lagonoy Gulf, making it home to a variety of marine species. Common catches include Tamban (Sardine), Tulingan (Tuna), Bisugo (Threadfin Bream), and Barongoy (Flying Fish). The region also supports iconic species like Butanding (Whale Shark) in Sorsogon and Taklobo (Giant Clams) in its coral reefs, contributing to both livelihoods and marine biodiversity.

NCR

Despite its urban setting, NCR is connected to important fishing grounds like Manila Bay and Laguna de Bay. It supports small-scale fisheries with species such as Tilapia, Bangus (Milkfish), Dilis (Anchovy), and Tamban (Sardine). These species remain vital for food supply and local markets across Metro Manila.

CAR

CAR is a highland region with freshwater rivers, streams, and lakes that support native and introduced species. Commonly found are Dalag (Mudfish), Tilapia, Carpa (Carp), Martiniko (Climbing Perch), and Bungto (Native Goby). These freshwater species are an essential part of local food culture and rural livelihoods in the mountains.

Luzon Fish Species

Discover the diverse aquatic life of the northern Philippines

About Luzon's Marine Biodiversity

Luzon, the largest island in the Philippines, is surrounded by several bodies of water including the West Philippine Sea and Sibuyan Sea. This geographical advantage contributes to its rich marine biodiversity, hosting hundreds of fish species that are both economically and ecologically important.

The waters around Luzon are home to various habitats including coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrass beds, which support diverse fish populations. From the deep-sea fishes of Batanes to the freshwater species of Laguna de Bay, Luzon offers a fascinating glimpse into the Philippines' aquatic treasures.

Ilocos Region

The Ilocos Region, located in the northwestern part of Luzon, is known for its rich coastal waters that support both artisanal and commercial fishing activities.

Dilis (Anchovy)

Small but economically important, dilis is often dried and used as a condiment or side dish.

Location: Pangasinan coastline

Galunggong (Roundscad)

Decapterus macrosoma

A staple fish in Filipino diets, known locally as "Galunggong," and a significant catch in many parts of the Philippines, including the Ilocos Region.

Location: Coastal waters of the Ilocos Region

Skipjack Tuna (Gulyasan)

Katsuwonus pelamis

An important pelagic tuna species commonly caught in the waters off Luzon, including the Ilocos coast.

Location: Offshore waters of the Ilocos Region

Groupers (Lapu-Lapu)

Epinephelidae

High-value reef fish, known locally as "Lapu-Lapu," found in the coral reefs and rocky areas along the Ilocos coast.

Location: Coral reefs and rocky areas off the Ilocos coast

Malaga (Siganid)

A popular fish in Ilocano cuisine, often prepared as kilawin (ceviche) or inabraw (fish soup).

Location: Ilocos Norte, Ilocos Sur

Snappers (Maya-Maya)

Lutjanus lutjanus

Commercially important reef fish, known locally as "Maya-Maya" or other names, inhabiting the reefs off the Ilocos coast.

Location: Coral reefs off the Ilocos coast

Cavalla (Talakitok)

Carangidae

Popular food and game fish found in the coastal waters of the Ilocos Region.

Location: Coastal waters of the Ilocos Region

Yellowfin Tuna (Tambakol)

Thunnus albacares

Another valuable tuna species found in the broader Philippine waters, including areas accessible to Ilocano fishers.

Location: Offshore waters accessible from the Ilocos Region

Tilapia

Widely farmed in the freshwater bodies of the region, tilapia is an important source of protein for local communities.

Location: Paoay Lake, La Union

Cagayan Valley

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Alumahan

Rastrelliger kanagurta & Scomber japonicus

Smaller mackerel species that are likely present in the coastal and offshore waters.

Location: Coastal waters of Ballesteros (Cagayan) and Ivana (Batanes)

Angelfish (Espada)

Centropyge loricula

Colorful marine angelfish known for their flattened bodies and graceful movements.

Location: Coral gardens near Piat (Cagayan) and Basco’s reef zones (Batanes)

dilis (Anchovy)

Exocoetus volitans

Fish with enlarged pectoral fins that allow them to glide above the water’s surface.

Location: Coastal waters off Pamplona (Cagayan) and offshore Basco (Batanes)

Flying Fish (Barongoy / Bangsi)

Various Stolephorus spp. and other Engraulidae

Small, schooling fish often found near the coast, used in various culinary preparations. Also known as Tiki-Tiki/Bolinao.

Location: Coastal waters off Cagayan and Batanes

Dorado / Mahi-Mahi (Dorado)

Coryphaena hippurus

A fast-growing and colorful pelagic fish found in tropical and subtropical surface waters.

Location: Offshore waters around Palaui Island (Cagayan) and Sabtang Channel (Batanes)

Groupers (Lapu-Lapu)

Epinephelus coioides & Epinephelus malabaricus

Reef-associated fish, highly valued for their culinary qualities.

Location: Coral reefs around Claveria (Cagayan) and Sabtang Island (Batanes)

Blue Marlin (Malasugi)

Makaira nigricans

A large and majestic billfish, a prized game fish found in the open ocean.

Location: Deep oceanic waters east of Sta. Ana (Cagayan) and southeast of Basco (Batanes)

Snappers (Maya-Maya)

Lutjanus argentimaculatus & Lutjanus monostigma

Commercially important reef fish with firm, flavorful flesh.

Location: Reef areas off Santa Praxedes (Cagayan) and near Mahatao (Batanes)

Parrotfish (Molmol)

Scarus ghobban & Chlorurus sordidus

Herbivorous fish with beak-like mouths, important for controlling algae on reefs.

Location: Coral reef flats off Abulug (Cagayan) and Diura Fishing Village (Batanes)

Sailfish (Palata)

Istiophorus platypterus

A billfish characterized by its prominent sail-like dorsal fin, inhabiting pelagic waters.

Location: Open sea off Gonzaga (Cagayan) and Mavulis Island (Batanes)

Butterflyfish (Paru-Paro)

Chaetodon auriga & Chaetodon kleinii

Small, often brightly colored reef fish with diverse patterns.

Location: Coral-rich zones of Buguey Lagoon (Cagayan) and Marlboro Hills reefs (Batanes)

Skipjack Tuna (Gulyasan)

Katsuwonus pelamis

A migratory pelagic tuna, often found schooling with other tuna species in the open ocean.

Location: Deep offshore areas near Calayan Island (Cagayan) and Sabtang (Batanes)

Jacks / Trevally (Talakitok)

Caranx ignobilis & Caranx melampygus

Active predatory fish often associated with reefs and coastal areas.

Location: Reefs and rocky coasts off Camiguin Island (Cagayan) and Ivana (Batanes)

Yellowfin Tuna (Tambakol)

Thunnus albacares

A migratory pelagic tuna species highly valued for its meat, found in the open waters off Cagayan and Batanes.

Location: Offshore waters of Sta. Ana (Cagayan) and Itbayat (Batanes)

Sardines (Tamban)

Sardinella lemuru & Sardinella gibbosa

Small, schooling oily fish found in coastal waters, important for local fisheries.

Location: Coastal waters of Ballesteros (Cagayan) and Sabtang Island (Batanes)

Spanish Mackerel (Tanguigue)

Scomberomorus commerson

A large mackerel species known for its firm texture and rich flavor, inhabiting pelagic waters.

Location: Offshore waters of Buguey Bay (Cagayan) and near Itbayat Island (Batanes)

Frigate Tuna (Tulingan)

Auxis thazard

A smaller tuna species commonly found in tropical and subtropical waters.

Location: Pelagic zones off Aparri (Cagayan) and around Basco (Batanes)

Central Luzon

Central Luzon is home to Manila Bay and several important aquaculture areas, including the fishponds of Pampanga and Bulacan.

Alumahan (Indian Mackerel)

Rastrelliger kanagurta

A common medium-sized mackerel found in schools, important for local consumption and commercial fisheries in the region. Also known as Mackerel or Salay-Salay.

Location: Manila Bay, West Philippine Sea (off the coast of Central Luzon)

Bangus (Milkfish)

Chanos chanos

The most important cultured fish in the Philippines, with Pampanga and Bulacan being major producers.

Location: Pampanga, Bulacan

Butanding (Whale Shark)

Rhincodon typus

The world's largest fish, known for its gentle nature and filter-feeding habits. While sightings are less frequent than in areas like Donsol or Cebu, they can occasionally be observed in the open waters off Central Luzon during their migrations.

Location: Offshore waters of Central Luzon (West Philippine Sea)

Bolinao (Anchovy)

Stolephorus spp.

Small, slender fish often used in local cuisine for their distinct flavor. They form large schools and are caught using various methods. Also known as Dilis or Tiki-Tiki.

Location: Manila Bay, coastal waters of Central Luzon

Lapu-Lapu (Grouper)

Epinephelidae

A group of high-value reef fish prized for their firm, white flesh. Various species can be found in the coral reefs and rocky areas along the coast.

Location: Coastal areas and reefs of Central Luzon (e.g., Zambales, Bataan)

Maya-Maya (Snapper)

Lutjanus lutjanus

Another group of commercially important reef fish known for their excellent taste and texture. Different snapper species inhabit the waters off Central Luzon.

Location: Coastal areas and reefs of Central Luzon (e.g., Zambales, Bataan)

Pawikan (Sea Turtle)

Chelonia mydas, Eretmochelys imbricata

Endangered marine reptiles that nest on some beaches in Central Luzon and forage in its coastal waters. They play a crucial role in marine ecosystems.

Location: Coastal waters and nesting beaches of Central Luzon (e.g., Zambales, Bataan)

Tamban (Sardine)

Sardinella spp.

Small, oily schooling fish that are a vital part of the marine food web and are commercially important. Often caught in large quantities. Also known as Sardines.

Location: West Philippine Sea (off the coast of Central Luzon), Manila Bay

CALABARZON

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Bisugo (Threadfin Bream)

Nemipterus spp.

Small, pinkish fish with sweet-tasting meat, commonly found in coastal fishing grounds.

Location: Shelf areas of Batangas and Quezon coasts.

Butanding (Whale Shark)

Rhincodon typus

The world's largest fish, known for its gentle nature. While not a permanent resident, whale sharks are known to visit the waters off Quezon and Batangas during their migrations, attracting researchers and tourists.

Location: Offshore waters of Quezon and Batangas during migration periods.

Siganid (Spotted Rabbitfish)

Siganus guttatus

A commercially important rabbitfish species often found in seagrass beds and coral reef areas. It's a popular food fish in coastal communities.

Location: Coastal waters of Batangas, especially areas with seagrass and shallow reefs.

Dilis (Anchovy)

Stolephorus spp. and other Engraulidae

Small, schooling fish found near the coast, often used for fish paste and other culinary purposes.

Location: Coastal waters of Cavite, Batangas, and Quezon, including Manila Bay.

Maliputo (Giant Trevally in Taal Lake)

Caranx ignobilis

A prized game fish found in Taal Lake, Batangas, known for its delicious meat and fighting spirit. It's the same species as the marine Giant Trevally but has adapted to freshwater.

Location: Taal Lake, Batangas (Note: A freshwater adaptation of a marine species, iconic to Taal Lake)

Matangbaka(big eyescad)

Selar crumenophthalmus

A schooling pelagic fish often found in coastal waters and around reefs. It's an important small-scale fishery resource.

Location: Coastal waters of Batangas and Quezon.

Anilao's Nudibranchs

Various species within the Opisthobranchia order

The Anilao area in Batangas is world-renowned for its high diversity of colorful nudibranchs (sea slugs). These small, often vibrant creatures are a major attraction for scuba divers and underwater photographers.

Location: Coral reefs of Anilao, Batangas.

Pawikan(sea turtles)

Chelonia mydas

These majestic reptiles nest on the beaches of CALABARZON, particularly in Quezon and parts of Batangas. They are important for marine ecosystems and are a focus of conservation efforts.

Location: Coastal nesting beaches of Quezon and Batangas.

Sapsap(slipmouths)">

Leiognathidae

Small, silvery fish with protrusible mouths, common in shallow coastal waters and estuaries, including Manila Bay and other parts of CALABARZON.

Location: Manila Bay, coastal estuaries and shallow waters of Cavite, Batangas, and Quezon.

Taklobo(Giant Clams)

Tridacna spp.

Large bivalve mollusks found in the coral reefs of Batangas and Quezon. They are ecologically important and can be quite striking in appearance.

Location: Coral reefs of Batangas and Quezon.

Tawilis

Sardinella tawilis

A small freshwater sardine endemic to Taal Lake in Batangas. It holds cultural and ecological significance as the only freshwater sardine in the world.

Location: Taal Lake, Batangas (Note: While primarily freshwater, its unique nature makes it iconic to the region)

MIMAROPA

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Bulaklak ng Dagat

Acanthaster planci

While sometimes considered a threat to coral reefs in high densities, it is a native species and a significant part of the ecosystem. Its presence is notable in the extensive reefs of MIMAROPA.

Location: Coral reefs throughout MIMAROPA.

Butanding (Whale Shark)

Rhincodon typus

The largest fish in the world, these gentle giants filter-feed on plankton. They are known to aggregate in certain areas of the Philippines, including waters accessible from MIMAROPA.

Location: Open waters off Palawan and potentially other parts of MIMAROPA during migration periods.

Danggit (Rabbitfish)

Siganus spp.

A popular breakfast fish, often dried, abundant in the shallow waters around Marinduque and Mindoro, associated with seagrass beds and reefs.

Location: Shallow waters around Marinduque and Mindoro.

Dugong

Dugong dugon

A gentle, herbivorous marine mammal that grazes on seagrass beds. Palawan is a significant habitat for this vulnerable species in the Philippines.

Location: Seagrass beds around Palawan, as well as parts of Mindoro and Romblon.

Galunggong (Roundscad)

Decapterus macrosoma

A staple fish in Filipino diets, caught in various parts of MIMAROPA.

Location: Coastal waters throughout MIMAROPA.

Pawikan (Green Sea Turtle)

Chelonia mydas

A large sea turtle species that feeds on seagrass and algae. MIMAROPA hosts important nesting sites and foraging grounds for green sea turtles.

Location: Nesting beaches and coastal waters of Palawan, Mindoro (e.g., Sablayan), and other islands in the region.

Lapu-Lapu (Grouper)

Epinephelidae genera

High-value reef fish prized for their firm, white flesh, abundant in the extensive coral reefs of Palawan and other islands in MIMAROPA.

Location: Coral reefs throughout Palawan, Mindoro, Marinduque, and Romblon.

Lumod (Irrawaddy Dolphin)

Orcaella brevirostris

A small, toothed whale found in coastal areas and estuaries. While not strictly marine, they inhabit brackish waters connected to the sea. A population is known to exist in Malampaya Sound, Palawan.

Location: Malampaya Sound, Palawan.

Maya-Maya (Snapper)

Lutjanidae

Premium fish with firm white flesh, commonly found in the waters around Palawan and Romblon, associated with reefs and deeper fishing grounds.

Location: Waters around Palawan and Romblon.

Pagi (Stingray)

Myliobatoidei

Graceful filter-feeding rays often seen gliding through the water. They are known to frequent reef areas in Palawan.

Location: Reefs and open waters around Palawan.

Pawikan (Hawksbill Sea Turtle)

Eretmochelys imbricata

A critically endangered sea turtle with a distinctive hooked beak, often found in coral reef areas feeding on sponges and other invertebrates.

Location: Coral reefs throughout Palawan, Romblon, and other parts of MIMAROPA.

Taklobo (Giant Clams)

Tridacna gigas

The largest living bivalve mollusk, found in coral reefs. Palawan is known for its populations of giant clams, some of which are part of conservation efforts.

Location: Coral reefs of Palawan and other islands in MIMAROPA.

Tambakol (Mackerel)

Scomberomorus spp.

Commercially important pelagic fish found in the waters of MIMAROPA.

Location: Coastal and offshore waters of MIMAROPA.

Tanguigue (Spanish Mackerel)

Scomberomorus commerson

A larger mackerel species favored for its firm texture and rich flavor.

Location: Waters around Palawan and Romblon.

Bicol Region

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Alumahan (Indian Mackerel)

Rastrelliger kanagurta

A staple in wet markets, commonly grilled or fried. Recognized for its affordability and rich flavor.

Location: Albay coast

Barongoy (Flying Fish)

Exocoetidae

Known for gliding above the water surface, this fish is unique to deeper open waters and caught near the Sulu Sea and parts of eastern Bicol.

Location: Eastern Bicol Coast (Sorsogon, Albay)

Bisugo (Threadfin Bream)

Nemipteridae

A common market fish in Bicol with a pinkish hue and mild, sweet-tasting meat, frequently used in soups or grilled dishes..

Location: Sorsogon Bay, Albay Gulf

Butanding (Whale Shark)

Rhincodon typus

The world's largest fish, a gentle giant filter-feeder that aggregates in significant numbers in Donsol, Sorsogon, making it a globally renowned site for interaction.

Location: Donsol, Sorsogon

Danggit (Rabbitfish)

Siganus

A popular breakfast fish, often dried and paired with vinegar or garlic rice. Found in shallow coastal waters and coral reefs.

Location: Sorsogon, Albay

Lumod (Irrawaddy Dolphin)

Orcaella brevirostris

A critically endangered dolphin species that inhabits coastal waters and estuaries. While their presence might be less widely known than whale sharks, they have been reported in certain areas of the Philippines

Location: Coastal estuaries and waters of the Bicol region

Pagi (Stingray)

Myliobatoidei

Large, graceful filter-feeding rays known for their wing-like pectoral fins. They are often sighted in the waters around the Bicol region, including near Ticao Island.

Location: Waters off Ticao Island (Masbate, often associated with the Bicol region), coastal areas of Albay and Sorsogon.

Pawikan (Sea Turtles)

Chelonia mydas

Endangered marine reptiles that nest on the beaches and forage in the seagrass beds and coral reefs of the Bicol region.

Location: Nesting beaches and coastal waters of Albay and Sorsogon.

Pusit (Squid)

Sepioteuthis lessoniana

A widely consumed cephalopod in the region, often grilled or used in adobo. Actively fished in the deeper parts of the Bicol seas.

Location: Albay Gulf, Sorsogon waters

Sinarapan (Tabios)

Mistichthys luzonensis

Known as the world's smallest commercially harvested fish, this tiny species is endemic to Lake Buhi in Camarines Sur and is considered a delicacy.

Location: Lake Buhi, Camarines Sur

Taklobo (Giant Clams)

Tridacna gigas

Large bivalve mollusks that inhabit coral reefs. Several species can be found in the reef ecosystems of the Bicol region, some of which are endangered.

Location: Coral reefs of Albay and Sorsogon.

Tamban (Sardine)

Sardina pilchardus

Small, oily fish that often swim in large schools. They are a significant part of the marine food web and are widely consumed, often canned.

Location: Aklan, Antique, Leyte, Samar, Manila Bay

Tiki-tiki (Anchovy)

Engraulidae

Tiny schooling fish widely used in making bagoong or dried fish products. Common in coastal Bicol towns.

Location: Sorsogon, Camarines Sur.

Tulingan (Mackerel Tuna)

SEuthynnus affinis

A medium-sized tuna species that’s important to regional fisheries. Known for its strong flavor and is often used in local dishes like sinaing or paksiw.

Location: Lagonoy Gulf, Albay

NCR

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Alumahan (Indian Mackerel)

Rastrelliger kanagurta

A common fish in Metro Manila markets, often sourced from nearby provinces but also caught in Manila Bay. Also known as Mackerel or Salay-Salay.

Location: Manila Bay, Northern Samar, Eastern Samar, Davao Gulf

Bangus (Milkfish)

Chanos chanos

The most important cultured fish in the Philippines. Metro Manila is a major market for Bangus, sourced from Pampanga and Bulacan.

Location: Pampanga, Bulacan, Cebu, Bohol, Leyte, Samar, Metro Manila (markets)

Dalagang Bukid (Yellowtail Fusilier)

Pterocaesio chrysozona

Known for its vibrant colors and delicate flavor, often sold in wet markets in Metro Manila and cooked in sinigang or fried.

Location: Manila Bay, Southern Luzon, Visayas

Dilis (Anchovy)

Stolephorus spp.

Very small, salty fish usually dried and eaten as snacks or side dishes with rice. A staple in markets and households in NCR.

Location: Manila Bay, various coastal areas

Hasa-hasa (Short-bodied Mackerel)

Rastrelliger brachysoma

A smaller species of mackerel commonly found in tropical waters. It's a popular and affordable food fish in the Philippines. Also known as Matambaka (Mackerel Scad).

Location: Leyte, Samar, Misamis Oriental, Camiguin, Manila Bay

Pusit (Squid)

Sepioteuthis lessoniana

Cephalopods with elongated bodies, large eyes, and ten arms. A popular seafood enjoyed grilled, fried, or stuffed.

Location: Leyte Gulf, Eastern Samar, Manila Bay

Tamban (Sardine)

Sardina pilchardus

Small, oily fish that often swim in large schools. They are a significant part of the marine food web and are widely consumed, often canned.

Location: Aklan, Antique, Leyte, Samar, Manila Bay

Tiki-tiki (Anchovy)

Engraulidae

Small, schooling fish with a salty and umami flavor. They are often used in sauces, pastes, or eaten whole. Also known as Bolinao.

Location: Leyte, Samar, Iligan Bay, Manila Bay

Tilapia

Oreochromis

Another farmed fish that's widely available in Metro Manila markets, tilapia is an affordable source of protein for many urban dwellers. Widely farmed in various regions.

Location: Metro Manila (farmed), Ambuklao Dam, Baguio City

Tulingan (Mackerel Tuna)

Euthynnus affinis

A medium-sized tuna species that's commercially important in the region. Often caught in coastal waters and sold in wet markets. Also known as Skipjack Tuna.

Location: Lagonoy Gulf, Manila Bay, Bohol, Celebes Sea

CAR

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Bungto (Native Goby)

Glossogobius spp.

Small native gobies that inhabit mountain streams and rivers. They are part of the local food tradition in remote Cordilleran towns.

Location: Abra, Ifugao, Benguet

Carpa (Common Carp)

Cyprinus carpio

Introduced to the Philippines, this freshwater fish is now common in the rivers and lakes of the Cordillera region. Also known as Tëmaginting (Silver Carp).

Location: Chico River, Mountain Province, Maguindanao

Dalag (Mudfish)

Channa striata

A resilient freshwater species found in rice fields and swamps. Valued for its ability to survive in low-oxygen environments.

Location: Abra River, Kalinga

Palilang (Freshwater Shrimp)

Macrobrachium spp.

These river prawns are common in clean, flowing waters. They are often caught by hand or with bamboo traps.

Location: Abra, Apayao, Mountain Province

Igat (Eel)

Anguilla spp.

Freshwater eels that migrate between rivers and the sea. Considered a delicacy in some upland Cordillera towns.

Location: Kalinga, Abra River tributaries

Kanduli (sea Catfish)

Arius manillensis

A native catfish species that thrives in river systems of the Cordillera. Often cooked in local sour stews.

Location: Chico River, Apayao

Kuhol (Freshwater Snail)

Pomacea canaliculata

While often considered a pest in rice fields, kuhol is also harvested as a food source in the region and cooked in coconut milk.

Location: Rice fields and waterways in Kalinga and Ifugao

Martiniko (Climbing Perch)

Anabas testudineus

A hardy freshwater fish that can breathe air and survive out of water temporarily. Often caught in paddies and creeks.

Location: Mountain Province, Ifugao

Tambaki (Black Pacu)

Colossoma macropomum

A South American species introduced to Philippine aquaculture. Known for its fast growth and adaptability to upland fishponds.

Location: Aquaculture sites in Benguet and Ifugao

Tilapia

Oreochromis

A widely farmed freshwater fish in Ambuklao Dam and nearby areas. Known for being a low-cost protein source in local communities.

Location: Ambuklao Dam, Baguio City

Luzon Fish Species

Discover the diverse aquatic life of the northern Philippines

About Luzon's Marine Biodiversity

Luzon, the largest island in the Philippines, is surrounded by several bodies of water including the West Philippine Sea and Sibuyan Sea. This geographical advantage contributes to its rich marine biodiversity, hosting hundreds of fish species that are both economically and ecologically important.

The waters around Luzon are home to various habitats including coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrass beds, which support diverse fish populations. From the deep-sea fishes of Batanes to the freshwater species of Laguna de Bay, Luzon offers a fascinating glimpse into the Philippines' aquatic treasures.

Ilocos Region

The Ilocos Region, located in the northwestern part of Luzon, is known for its rich coastal waters that support both artisanal and commercial fishing activities.

Dilis (Anchovy)

Small but economically important, dilis is often dried and used as a condiment or side dish.

Location: Pangasinan coastline

Galunggong (Roundscad)

Decapterus macrosoma

A staple fish in Filipino diets, known locally as "Galunggong," and a significant catch in many parts of the Philippines, including the Ilocos Region.

Location: Coastal waters of the Ilocos Region

Skipjack Tuna (Gulyasan)

Katsuwonus pelamis

An important pelagic tuna species commonly caught in the waters off Luzon, including the Ilocos coast.

Location: Offshore waters of the Ilocos Region

Groupers (Lapu-Lapu)

Epinephelidae

High-value reef fish, known locally as "Lapu-Lapu," found in the coral reefs and rocky areas along the Ilocos coast.

Location: Coral reefs and rocky areas off the Ilocos coast

Malaga (Siganid)

A popular fish in Ilocano cuisine, often prepared as kilawin (ceviche) or inabraw (fish soup).

Location: Ilocos Norte, Ilocos Sur

Snappers (Maya-Maya)

Lutjanus lutjanus

Commercially important reef fish, known locally as "Maya-Maya" or other names, inhabiting the reefs off the Ilocos coast.

Location: Coral reefs off the Ilocos coast

Cavalla (Talakitok)

Carangidae

Popular food and game fish found in the coastal waters of the Ilocos Region.

Location: Coastal waters of the Ilocos Region

Yellowfin Tuna (Tambakol)

Thunnus albacares

Another valuable tuna species found in the broader Philippine waters, including areas accessible to Ilocano fishers.

Location: Offshore waters accessible from the Ilocos Region

Tilapia

Widely farmed in the freshwater bodies of the region, tilapia is an important source of protein for local communities.

Location: Paoay Lake, La Union

Cagayan Valley

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Alumahan

Rastrelliger kanagurta & Scomber japonicus

Smaller mackerel species that are likely present in the coastal and offshore waters.

Location: Coastal waters of Ballesteros (Cagayan) and Ivana (Batanes)

Angelfish (Espada)

Centropyge loricula

Colorful marine angelfish known for their flattened bodies and graceful movements.

Location: Coral gardens near Piat (Cagayan) and Basco’s reef zones (Batanes)

dilis (Anchovy)

Exocoetus volitans

Fish with enlarged pectoral fins that allow them to glide above the water’s surface.

Location: Coastal waters off Pamplona (Cagayan) and offshore Basco (Batanes)

Flying Fish (Barongoy / Bangsi)

Various Stolephorus spp. and other Engraulidae

Small, schooling fish often found near the coast, used in various culinary preparations. Also known as Tiki-Tiki/Bolinao.

Location: Coastal waters off Cagayan and Batanes

Dorado / Mahi-Mahi (Dorado)

Coryphaena hippurus

A fast-growing and colorful pelagic fish found in tropical and subtropical surface waters.

Location: Offshore waters around Palaui Island (Cagayan) and Sabtang Channel (Batanes)

Groupers (Lapu-Lapu)

Epinephelus coioides & Epinephelus malabaricus

Reef-associated fish, highly valued for their culinary qualities.

Location: Coral reefs around Claveria (Cagayan) and Sabtang Island (Batanes)

Blue Marlin (Malasugi)

Makaira nigricans

A large and majestic billfish, a prized game fish found in the open ocean.

Location: Deep oceanic waters east of Sta. Ana (Cagayan) and southeast of Basco (Batanes)

Snappers (Maya-Maya)

Lutjanus argentimaculatus & Lutjanus monostigma

Commercially important reef fish with firm, flavorful flesh.

Location: Reef areas off Santa Praxedes (Cagayan) and near Mahatao (Batanes)

Parrotfish (Molmol)

Scarus ghobban & Chlorurus sordidus

Herbivorous fish with beak-like mouths, important for controlling algae on reefs.

Location: Coral reef flats off Abulug (Cagayan) and Diura Fishing Village (Batanes)

Sailfish (Palata)

Istiophorus platypterus

A billfish characterized by its prominent sail-like dorsal fin, inhabiting pelagic waters.

Location: Open sea off Gonzaga (Cagayan) and Mavulis Island (Batanes)

Butterflyfish (Paru-Paro)

Chaetodon auriga & Chaetodon kleinii

Small, often brightly colored reef fish with diverse patterns.

Location: Coral-rich zones of Buguey Lagoon (Cagayan) and Marlboro Hills reefs (Batanes)

Skipjack Tuna (Gulyasan)

Katsuwonus pelamis

A migratory pelagic tuna, often found schooling with other tuna species in the open ocean.

Location: Deep offshore areas near Calayan Island (Cagayan) and Sabtang (Batanes)

Jacks / Trevally (Talakitok)

Caranx ignobilis & Caranx melampygus

Active predatory fish often associated with reefs and coastal areas.

Location: Reefs and rocky coasts off Camiguin Island (Cagayan) and Ivana (Batanes)

Yellowfin Tuna (Tambakol)

Thunnus albacares

A migratory pelagic tuna species highly valued for its meat, found in the open waters off Cagayan and Batanes.

Location: Offshore waters of Sta. Ana (Cagayan) and Itbayat (Batanes)

Sardines (Tamban)

Sardinella lemuru & Sardinella gibbosa

Small, schooling oily fish found in coastal waters, important for local fisheries.

Location: Coastal waters of Ballesteros (Cagayan) and Sabtang Island (Batanes)

Spanish Mackerel (Tanguigue)

Scomberomorus commerson

A large mackerel species known for its firm texture and rich flavor, inhabiting pelagic waters.

Location: Offshore waters of Buguey Bay (Cagayan) and near Itbayat Island (Batanes)

Frigate Tuna (Tulingan)

Auxis thazard

A smaller tuna species commonly found in tropical and subtropical waters.

Location: Pelagic zones off Aparri (Cagayan) and around Basco (Batanes)

Central Luzon

Central Luzon is home to Manila Bay and several important aquaculture areas, including the fishponds of Pampanga and Bulacan.

Alumahan (Indian Mackerel)

Rastrelliger kanagurta

A common medium-sized mackerel found in schools, important for local consumption and commercial fisheries in the region. Also known as Mackerel or Salay-Salay.

Location: Manila Bay, West Philippine Sea (off the coast of Central Luzon)

Bangus (Milkfish)

The most important cultured fish in the Philippines, with Pampanga and Bulacan being major producers.

Location: Pampanga, Bulacan

Butanding (Whale Shark)

Rhincodon typus

The world's largest fish, known for its gentle nature and filter-feeding habits. While sightings are less frequent than in areas like Donsol or Cebu, they can occasionally be observed in the open waters off Central Luzon during their migrations.

Location: Offshore waters of Central Luzon (West Philippine Sea)

Bolinao (Anchovy)

Stolephorus spp.

Small, slender fish often used in local cuisine for their distinct flavor. They form large schools and are caught using various methods. Also known as Dilis or Tiki-Tiki.

Location: Manila Bay, coastal waters of Central Luzon

Lapu-Lapu (Grouper)

Epinephelidae

A group of high-value reef fish prized for their firm, white flesh. Various species can be found in the coral reefs and rocky areas along the coast.

Location: Coastal areas and reefs of Central Luzon (e.g., Zambales, Bataan)

Maya-Maya (Snapper)

Lutjanus lutjanus

Another group of commercially important reef fish known for their excellent taste and texture. Different snapper species inhabit the waters off Central Luzon.

Location: Coastal areas and reefs of Central Luzon (e.g., Zambales, Bataan)

Pawikan (Sea Turtle)

Chelonia mydas, Eretmochelys imbricata

Endangered marine reptiles that nest on some beaches in Central Luzon and forage in its coastal waters. They play a crucial role in marine ecosystems.

Location: Coastal waters and nesting beaches of Central Luzon (e.g., Zambales, Bataan)

Tamban (Sardine)

Sardinella spp.

Small, oily schooling fish that are a vital part of the marine food web and are commercially important. Often caught in large quantities. Also known as Sardines.

Location: West Philippine Sea (off the coast of Central Luzon), Manila Bay

CALABARZON

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Bisugo (Threadfin Bream)

Nemipterus spp.

Small, pinkish fish with sweet-tasting meat, commonly found in coastal fishing grounds.

Location: Shelf areas of Batangas and Quezon coasts.

Butanding (Whale Shark)

Rhincodon typus

The world's largest fish, known for its gentle nature. While not a permanent resident, whale sharks are known to visit the waters off Quezon and Batangas during their migrations, attracting researchers and tourists.

Location: Offshore waters of Quezon and Batangas during migration periods.

Siganid (Spotted Rabbitfish)

Siganus guttatus

A commercially important rabbitfish species often found in seagrass beds and coral reef areas. It's a popular food fish in coastal communities.

Location: Coastal waters of Batangas, especially areas with seagrass and shallow reefs.

Dilis (Anchovy)

Stolephorus spp. and other Engraulidae

Small, schooling fish found near the coast, often used for fish paste and other culinary purposes.

Location: Coastal waters of Cavite, Batangas, and Quezon, including Manila Bay.

Maliputo (Giant Trevally in Taal Lake)

Caranx ignobilis

A prized game fish found in Taal Lake, Batangas, known for its delicious meat and fighting spirit. It's the same species as the marine Giant Trevally but has adapted to freshwater.

Location: Taal Lake, Batangas (Note: A freshwater adaptation of a marine species, iconic to Taal Lake)

Matangbaka(big eyescad)

Selar crumenophthalmus

A schooling pelagic fish often found in coastal waters and around reefs. It's an important small-scale fishery resource.

Location: Coastal waters of Batangas and Quezon.

Anilao's Nudibranchs

Various species within the Opisthobranchia order

The Anilao area in Batangas is world-renowned for its high diversity of colorful nudibranchs (sea slugs). These small, often vibrant creatures are a major attraction for scuba divers and underwater photographers, making them iconic to the region's marine biodiversity.

Location: Coral reefs of Anilao, Batangas.

Pawikan(sea turtles)

Chelonia mydas

These majestic reptiles nest on the beaches of CALABARZON, particularly in Quezon and parts of Batangas. They are important for marine ecosystems and are a focus of conservation efforts.

Location: Coastal nesting beaches of Quezon and Batangas.

Sapsap(slipmouths)">

Leiognathidae

Small, silvery fish with protrusible mouths, common in shallow coastal waters and estuaries, including Manila Bay and other parts of CALABARZON.

Location: Manila Bay, coastal estuaries and shallow waters of Cavite, Batangas, and Quezon.

Taklobo(Giant Clams)

Tridacna spp.

Large bivalve mollusks found in the coral reefs of Batangas and Quezon. They are ecologically important and can be quite striking in appearance.

Location: Coral reefs of Batangas and Quezon.

Tawilis

Sardinella tawilis

A small freshwater sardine endemic to Taal Lake in Batangas. It holds cultural and ecological significance as the only freshwater sardine in the world.

Location: Taal Lake, Batangas (Note: While primarily freshwater, its unique nature makes it iconic to the region)

Bicol Region

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Ludong (President's Fish)

Known as the "President's Fish" due to its rarity and high value, this migratory fish is endemic to the Cagayan River.

Location: Cagayan River

Pacific Bluefin Tuna

These large, powerful fish are found in the deep waters off the eastern coast of Cagayan.

Location: Babuyan Channel

Dalag (Mudfish)

A freshwater fish that can survive in oxygen-poor environments, commonly found in rice fields and swamps.

Location: Isabela wetlands

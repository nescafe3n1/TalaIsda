About Visayas' Marine Biodiversity

The Visayas region, located in the central part of the Philippines, is composed of several islands surrounded by rich marine waters. The Visayan Sea, one of the country's major fishing grounds, is home to a diverse array of fish species that support both local consumption and commercial fishing industries.

With its strategic location at the heart of the Coral Triangle, the Visayas boasts some of the most biodiverse marine ecosystems in the world. From the colorful coral reefs of Cebu and Bohol to the deep waters of Negros and Panay, the region offers a fascinating glimpse into the Philippines' underwater treasures.

Western Visayas

Western Visayas encompasses the islands of Panay, Guimaras, and the western part of Negros. The region is known for its rich fishing grounds, particularly in the Visayan Sea and Guimaras Strait.

Danggit (Rabbit Fish)

Danggit (Rabbitfish)

A popular breakfast fish in the Visayas, often dried and served with vinegar. It's abundant in the shallow waters around Iloilo and Guimaras.

Location: Iloilo,Guimaras
Managat (Red Snapper)

Managat (Red Snapper)

A premium fish with firm white flesh, commonly found in the deeper waters around Panay Island.

Location: Antique, Capiz
Kitong (Rabbitfish)

Kitong (Rabbitfish)

A relative of danggit but larger in size, this fish is a staple in many Ilonggo dishes.

Location: Negros Occidental, Guimaras

Central Visayas

Central Visayas includes the islands of Cebu, Bohol, Siquijor, and the eastern part of Negros. The region is famous for its marine sanctuaries and diving spots that showcase its rich underwater biodiversity.

Mamsa (Jack)

Mamsa (Jack)

A fast-swimming pelagic fish that's popular for its firm meat and versatility in cooking.

Location: Cebu Strait, Tañon Strait
Mol-mol (Parrotfish)

Mol-mol (Parrotfish)

Known for its colorful appearance and important role in coral reef ecosystems, this fish is a common sight in the waters of Bohol and Cebu.

Location: Bohol, Cebu
Pugapo (Grouper)

Pugapo (Grouper)

A high-value fish that's often served in upscale restaurants, pugapo is found in the coral reefs around Siquijor and southern Cebu.

Location: Siquijor, Southern Cebu

Eastern Visayas

Eastern Visayas comprises the islands of Samar, Leyte, and Biliran. The region faces the Philippine Sea and is known for its rich marine resources, particularly in the waters of San Bernardino Strait and Leyte Gulf.

Sapsap (Ponyfish)

Sapsap (Ponyfish)

A small, silvery fish that's abundant in the coastal waters of Samar and Leyte, often used in soups and stews.

Location: Samar, Leyte
Tambakol (Mackerel Tuna)

Tambakol (Mackerel Tuna)

A medium-sized tuna species that's commercially important in the region, often caught in the waters of San Bernardino Strait.

Location: Northern Samar
Bisugo (Threadfin Bream)

Bisugo (Threadfin Bream)

A common fish in local markets, known for its pinkish color and sweet-tasting meat.

Location: Leyte Gulf, Maqueda Bay

About Visayas' Marine Biodiversity

The Visayas, a central group of islands in the Philippines, is surrounded by some of the most biodiverse marine ecosystems in the world, including the Visayan Sea, the Bohol Sea, and the Cebu Strait. These rich waters make the Visayas a key region for marine biodiversity, supporting a wide variety of fish species crucial for local fisheries and ecological balance.

From the vibrant coral reefs in the Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park to the diverse marine life in the waters of Bohol and Leyte, the Visayas' seas are home to a wealth of marine wonders. Its extensive mangrove forests, seagrass meadows, and shallow coastal waters provide essential habitats for reef fish, migratory species, and important breeding grounds for marine life.

Western Visayas

Western Visayas, located along the Sibuyan Sea and the Panay Gulf, is renowned for its rich marine biodiversity, supporting both small-scale fishing communities and large commercial fisheries, particularly in areas like Iloilo and Negros Occidental.

Central Visayas

Cagayan Valley is blessed with the Cagayan River, the longest river in the Philippines, as well as coastal areas facing the Pacific Ocean. This region is known for its diverse freshwater and marine fish species.

Eastern Visayas

Eastern Visayas, bordered by the Leyte Gulf and the Philippine Sea, is known for its productive fishing grounds, which are vital to the region's local fishing industry and food security, particularly in Leyte and Samar.
